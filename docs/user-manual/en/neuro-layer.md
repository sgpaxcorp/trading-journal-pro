# Neuro Layer

## Access
- Open a Journal date.
- The Neuro Layer appears inside the Journal as part of the normal daily workflow.
- You do not need to open a separate tool.

## What Neuro Layer is
Neuro Layer is the decision and psychology layer of the Journal.

It turns one session into structured data about:
- what you planned before the trade
- what changed while the trade was live
- what state of mind you were in
- why you exited
- what the honest truth was after the trade

This is how the platform begins to understand your real process, not only your P&L.

## Why it exists
Most journals record:
- entries
- exits
- profit and loss
- notes

Neuro Layer adds the missing part:
- decision quality
- cognitive drift
- emotional state
- truth after the trade

This is what powers:
- Neuro Score
- Neuro Insight
- Neuro Memory
- deeper AI Coaching

## Important rule
Neuro Layer is designed to stay light.

You should mostly use:
- quick chips
- short selections
- one short truth sentence

It is not meant to become another long form you have to write every day.

## How it fits inside the Journal
Neuro Layer is built from three places in the same Journal:

1. `Premarket Prep`
2. `Inside the Trade`
3. `After-trade Analysis`

The platform reads those selections together as one decision story.

Each Neuro chip group can also be customized. Use `Customize` inside the Journal and add the chips that match your own language, setups, and repeatable patterns.

## Recommended fill order
Use this order every day:

1. Write or record your normal `Premarket Prep`.
2. Before the session starts, select the `Premarket` Neuro chips.
3. During the trade or right after it ends, mark the `Inside the Trade` chips.
   Also mark the live trade management actions that actually happened.
4. Complete the `After-trade` chips after the trade closes.
5. Write `One-line Truth` last.

This order matters because the platform compares what you planned, what changed, and what you later admitted was true.

## Core rule when filling it
Do not use Neuro Layer to defend the trade.

Use it to record:
- what the setup was
- what had to happen
- what changed
- what state you were in
- what the honest truth was

If you use it to protect your ego, the data becomes weak and AI Coaching becomes less accurate.

## Premarket Neuro checks
This section records what you believed before the trade.

### Thesis
Use this to mark the type of idea you were looking for.

Examples:
- Trend continuation
- Breakout
- Reversal
- Liquidity sweep
- Range fade
- Opening range breakout
- News reaction

Pick only what really matches the setup.

How to fill it:
- choose the main idea
- do not select every chip that sounds close
- if the idea was unclear, stay minimal instead of over-tagging

Good:
- `Breakout`
- `Trend continuation`

Weak:
- selecting 5 or 6 thesis chips for one simple trade
- choosing chips after the result to make the trade look planned

### Confirmation I need
Use this to define what had to happen before entry.

Examples:
- Level holds
- Reclaim
- Volume expansion
- VWAP reclaim
- Higher low
- Lower high
- Flow confirmation
- Market alignment

This matters because the platform later compares this against what you actually did.

How to fill it:
- choose the condition that truly had to happen before you entered
- if you entered without waiting, still mark the confirmation you were supposed to wait for
- this is about the required condition, not the outcome

Good:
- `VWAP reclaim`
- `Volume expansion`

Weak:
- choosing generic confirmations that had nothing to do with your trigger

### Invalidation
Use this to define what would break the trade idea.

Examples:
- Lose the level
- Fail reclaim
- No follow-through
- Tape weakens
- Flow disappears
- Against market trend

If you name invalidation clearly, the platform can judge process more accurately.

How to fill it:
- mark the condition that would make the trade idea wrong
- keep it tied to structure, flow, or market context
- if you did not define invalidation before the trade, do not invent one afterward

Good:
- `Lose the level`
- `No follow-through`

Weak:
- adding invalidation after the result only to sound disciplined

## Inside-the-Trade Neuro checks
This section records what happened while the trade was active.

### What changed?
Use this to mark the most important live change.

Examples:
- Nothing changed
- Market strengthened
- Market weakened
- Flow improved
- Flow faded
- Volatility expanded
- I entered early
- I hesitated
- I chased

### Current state
Use this to mark your real trading state, not the state you wish you had.

Examples:
- Calm
- Patient
- Clear
- Confident
- Hesitant
- Fearful
- Urgent
- Frustrated
- Revenge mode

These selections are used later for pattern recognition.

How to fill it:
- choose the real state, not the state you wish you had
- if more than one state happened, choose the one that most influenced the trade
- honesty here matters more than precision

### Did I follow the plan?
Choose one:
- Yes
- Partially
- No

This is one of the strongest inputs for process and drift analysis.

How to fill it:
- `Yes` = the trade stayed aligned with the original idea and management path
- `Partially` = part of the process held, but something meaningful bent
- `No` = the trade broke from the plan in a meaningful way

Do not answer from P&L.
Answer from process.

## After-trade Neuro checks
This section records how the trade ended and how honestly you reviewed it.

### Why did I exit?
Examples:
- Target hit
- Stop hit
- Structure broke
- Momentum faded
- Took profits early
- Emotional exit
- Risk reduction
- Manual close

### Would I take this trade again?
Choose one:
- Yes
- Only with changes
- No

This helps the platform separate a valid trade from a bad process trade.

How to fill it:
- `Yes` means the idea and process were valid enough to repeat
- `Only with changes` means something real must change before you repeat it
- `No` means you do not want to repeat that exact trade process

Do not answer from frustration alone. Answer from process quality.

### Truth about the trade
These are the truth tags.

Examples:
- Clean execution
- Valid idea, bad timing
- Good entry, bad exit
- Forced trade
- No clear edge
- Broke plan
- Managed well
- Emotion affected result

These tags matter because they shape Neuro Insight, Neuro Memory, and AI Coaching.

How to fill it:
- choose the tag that most honestly describes the trade
- if the trade made money but the process was poor, still mark `Forced trade` or `Broke plan` when true
- if the idea was valid but timing was poor, mark that honestly

This is where the platform learns your real leaks.

### One-line Truth
This is the shortest and most important sentence in the review.

Use one direct sentence such as:
- `I traded before confirmation.`
- `The setup was valid, but I managed it emotionally.`
- `I respected the plan, but exited too early.`

Keep it honest and short.

How to fill it:
- one sentence only
- shorter is better
- name the truth, not the story around it

Strong examples:
- `I entered before confirmation.`
- `I managed the winner emotionally.`
- `The setup was fine. My timing was not.`

Weak examples:
- long explanations
- blaming the market
- vague phrases like `bad day` or `market was weird`

## Custom Neuro tags
You can add your own repeatable patterns.

Use custom tags only for patterns that are truly yours.

Good examples:
- first trade rush
- overfocus on P&L
- late reclaim hesitation
- opening flush fakeout

Avoid creating many similar tags for the same idea.

How to fill them:
- use custom tags only for patterns that repeat
- if something happened once, do not make it a custom tag yet
- keep the name short and stable so the system can learn it over time

## Neuro Score
Neuro Score is the main visible score of the session.

Higher is better.

It starts at `100` and moves up or down based on the Neuro inputs you selected.

The platform only starts scoring when there are at least `3` Neuro signals. If there is not enough input yet, the score stays empty on purpose.

It is based on process signals such as:
- whether you named confirmation and invalidation
- whether you marked early entry or chasing
- whether you followed the plan
- whether the exit was emotional
- whether your truth tags show a forced trade or a broken plan
- whether your state stayed calm and patient

Neuro Score is not a profit score.
It is a process integrity score.

How to read it:
- higher = cleaner structure, less drift, more honest review
- lower = more mismatch between plan, execution, and truth

Do not ask:
- `Was the trade green?`

Ask:
- `Did my behavior stay aligned?`

## Neuro Insight
Neuro Insight is the short sentence the system gives you after reading the Neuro Layer.

It is shown:
- inside the Journal
- after save
- and can later be used by AI Coaching

It answers:
- where your decision process was strong
- where drift appeared
- what to protect next

How to use it:
- read it after save
- compare it against the chips you selected
- use it as a correction, not as a debate

## Decision Replay
Decision Replay is the summary view inside the Neuro Layer widget.

It shows the same trade in three stages:
- Premarket
- Inside
- After

This is the cleanest way to see whether your story stayed consistent.

How to use it:
- compare `Premarket` vs `Inside` vs `After`
- if the three sections tell different stories, that is usually a drift signal

## What cognitive drift means here
Cognitive drift means the trade changed from the plan in a meaningful way.

Examples:
- You marked confirmation in Premarket, then marked `I entered early` inside the trade.
- You marked a clear invalidation, but later marked `emotional exit`.
- You marked `forced trade`, but still answered `Yes` to taking the same trade again.

The platform uses this to detect drift. It does not use a drift label just because the trade lost money.

## Neuro Memory
Neuro Memory is a short pattern reminder shown on the Dashboard.

It does not summarize one day only.
It looks across recent Neuro-tagged sessions and surfaces one useful pattern.

Examples:
- repeated urgency
- repeated forced trades
- stable calm execution
- clear invalidation showing up in better sessions

Neuro Memory is there to remind you what your own data is already saying.

How to use it:
- read it before the next session
- treat it as a pattern reminder, not as a judgment
- if the same message keeps repeating, it means the pattern is persistent

## How AI Coaching uses Neuro Layer
AI Coaching reads Neuro Layer together with:
- Journal notes
- trades and timing
- Growth Plan
- analytics and KPIs
- execution data that already exists in the platform

This allows AI Coaching to compare:
- what you planned
- what you did
- what you later admitted was true

That is how the coach can speak more personally and more accurately.

What improves coaching quality:
- clear invalidation
- honest live-state chips
- truthful exit reasons
- strong One-line Truth
- stable custom tags for repeat patterns

What weakens coaching quality:
- random chip selection
- filling the tags after the fact to protect ego
- too many custom tags for the same issue

## Best practices
- Use chips first. Do not overwrite the same thing in text.
- Pick only the selections that truly fit the trade.
- Use One-line Truth even when the trade was good.
- Keep custom tags clean and repeatable.
- Do not use Neuro Layer to justify a trade. Use it to describe the trade honestly.

## FAQ
### Do I need to fill every chip?
No. Use only what matters.

### Should I repeat the same thing in text and in chips?
No. Use chips for structure and text only when extra context is needed.

### Is Neuro Score the same as performance?
No. A green trade can still have a weak Neuro Score if the process was poor.

### Can AI Coaching read this automatically?
Yes. AI Coaching reads Neuro Layer together with the rest of your Journal, Growth Plan, and analytics context.

### What is the minimum useful version?
At minimum, fill:
- one Thesis
- one Confirmation or Invalidation
- one Current state
- one Exit reason
- one Truth tag
- One-line Truth
