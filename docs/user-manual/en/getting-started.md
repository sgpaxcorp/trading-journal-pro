# Getting Started

## Access
- Help Center → Getting Started.

Neuro Trader Journal is a performance system. The fastest way to value is to complete one full loop: plan → execute → journal → review. This guide shows the exact sequence and the standard quality bar for each step.

## What “done” looks like
You have a saved Growth Plan, at least one completed trading session in the Journal, and a dashboard that reflects those sessions.

## 1) Create the account and confirm access
This unlocks the workspace and connects your profile to storage, reports, and AI coaching. Use the same email you plan to use long‑term, because that email anchors all imports, history, and entitlements.

## 2) Choose the plan that matches your workflow
Core is the disciplined journaling foundation. Advanced is for traders who need deeper analytics, reports, and AI coaching. If you expect to review performance weekly and make systematic changes, Advanced is the practical choice.

## 3) Build your Growth Plan (this is the system’s backbone)
Your plan is not a vanity target. It is the source of truth for progress, pacing, and risk control.

Set the following with intention:
1. Starting balance. Use the real account value you are trading from.
2. Target balance. Make it meaningful and measurable.
3. Target date. Choose a timeline you can honor.
4. Trading days. Define how many days you will actually trade.
5. Risk limits. Max daily loss and risk per trade must be realistic and enforceable.

Save the plan. The platform uses it to compute weekly and monthly checkpoints, goal pacing, and progress widgets.

## 4) Connect data: SnapTrade or CSV
Broker Sync (SnapTrade) is the fastest path. If your broker is not supported, use CSV import.

Best practice:
1. Connect or upload before you journal. Clean data creates accurate analytics.
2. Confirm the import completed successfully.
3. If using CSV, export the official trade history from your broker without edits.

## 5) Journal your first session
This is the most important habit. A session is complete when it includes:
1. Premarket plan. Bias, levels, and risk.
2. Execution notes. Entries, exits, sizing, and decisions.
3. Post‑trade review. What worked, what failed, what to repeat.

You are training decision quality, not just recording results.

## 6) Review Dashboard and Analytics
Your Dashboard shows behavior and compliance at a glance. Analytics shows the why.
1. Confirm P&L is aligned with your plan pace.
2. Review streaks and risk control.
3. Open Analytics to validate edge, time‑of‑day performance, and strategy breakdowns.

## 7) Use AI Coaching for feedback loops
AI coaching is most valuable after you have a minimum sample of sessions. Ask for a date range and request:
1. Patterns to reinforce.
2. Patterns to stop.
3. One specific rule to add or remove.

## 8) Option Flow (optional)
If you use the add‑on, generate the report and push the plan into your Journal so it becomes part of your premarket process.

## FAQ
Q: Why do my analytics look empty?  
A: Analytics requires saved sessions in the selected date range and a completed import.

Q: What should I do first every day?  
A: Review your plan, write your premarket notes, and confirm your max loss before the first trade.
