# Journal (Daily Session)

## Access
- Dashboard calendar → click a date to open its Journal.
- Or open Journal and select a date from the calendar.

The Journal is where execution becomes data. Each day captures your plan, actions, and review. A single date can include multiple sessions.

## Session structure (what “complete” looks like)
A complete session includes:
1. Premarket plan.
2. Entries and exits.
3. In‑trade notes.
4. Post‑trade review.

## Premarket Prep (plan before execution)
Use this section to define your intent.
Write:
1. Market bias and key levels.
2. The setup you are looking for.
3. Risk limits and a “do not break” rule.

## Day P&L
This block connects execution to outcomes.
- Auto P&L: pulls from your imported trades when available.
- Commissions: input the day’s total commissions.
- Fees: add exchange or broker fees.

This ensures net P&L is accurate.

## Entries (each entry you take)
Add each executed entry with full context.
Fields:
1. Symbol / Contract: the exact ticker or option contract.
2. Type: stock, option, future, etc.
3. Side: long or short.
4. Premium: debit or credit (options).
5. Option strategy: single, spread, etc.
6. Price: entry price.
7. Quantity: size of the entry.
8. Time: time of fill (use current time if needed).

The system calculates average entry price per symbol and shows DTE for options.

## Exits (close the position)
Close positions by selecting an open entry and defining the exit.
Fields:
1. Close position: pick the open position you want to exit.
2. Exit price: price of the close.
3. Qty to close: partials or full close.
4. Time: time of exit.

This drives accurate P&L and position tracking.

## Inside the Trade (execution notes)
Capture what happened while the trade was active:
- Why you entered.
- How you managed.
- Mistakes or rule breaks.
- Emotional state in real time.

Use dictation if you prefer speaking to typing.

## After‑Trade Analysis (review and correction)
Your review drives improvement.
Include:
1. What worked and what failed.
2. The correction you will apply next time.
3. Rule compliance and discipline notes.

Optional checklist + ratings:
- Exit execution
- Patience
- Mental clarity

## Mindset & Impulse (psychology tracking)
Use this to measure your mental state and impulse triggers.
- Emotional balance slider.
- Impulse control slider.
- State tags (e.g., calm, anxious, overconfident).
- Impulse triggers (e.g., revenge, FOMO, boredom).

Customize the tags to reflect your real patterns.

## Process & Strategy (your playbook)
This is the discipline layer.
1. Process phase checklist: premarket, in‑trade, or after‑trade steps.
2. Strategy checklist: confirms whether your setup qualified.
3. Setup quality rating.
4. Probability rating.
5. Exit evidence tags (why you exited).

## Templates (repeatable structure)
Save your best premarket/inside/after framework as a template. Apply it to future sessions to stay consistent.

## Best practices
- Log the session the same day. Accuracy drops the next day.
- Keep naming consistent (setups, tags, strategies).
- Short, honest notes are better than long, vague ones.

## FAQ
Q: Can I edit a Journal entry later?  
A: Yes. Open the same date and update fields.

Q: Do I need a Growth Plan first?  
A: Strongly recommended. It aligns your journal with clear goals and limits.
